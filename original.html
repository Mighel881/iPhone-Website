The original iPhone was a breakthrough in the mobile phone industry. Being able to touch the screen and interact
with content was incredible for its time. This was made better because Apple implemented Multi-Touch into the
display, allowing you too use multiple fingers on the display at one time.

The phone came preloaded with iPhone OS 1, and it didn't even come with the App Store. The App Store
was launched alongside the iPhone 3G in 2008. Although, the original iPhone did get an update to get the
App Store. Users of any phone had never had access to a store where they could download apps, and
Apple opened this up to the world.

When the iPhone was released, it became apparant that it was super locked-down compared to the soon-to-be-released Android. Along with
this came the Jailbreak community, a group of people who cracked iPhone OS so they could
install their own software. Along with Jailbreaking came Cydia, created by Jay Freeman (Saurik), a app that
allows all users to easily install Jailbreak tweaks.
