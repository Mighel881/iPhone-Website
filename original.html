The original iPhone was a breakthrough in the mobile phone industry. Being able to touch the screen and interact with
content was incredible for its time, however Apple chose to innovate even more and incorporated Multi-Touch into the
display. This allowed you too use multiple fingers on the display at one time.

The phone came preloaded with iPhone OS 1, but didn't include the App Store. The App Store
was launched alongside the iPhone 3G in 2008. The original iPhone recieved an update a few days later
that included the App Store. Users of any phone had never had access to a store where they could download apps, and
Apple opened this up to the world.

When the iPhone was released, it became apparant that it was super locked-down compared to the soon-to-be-released Android. As a result,
the jailbreak community started, a group of people who cracked iPhone OS (and later on, iOS) so they could
install their own software. Along with Jailbreaking came Cydia, created by Jay Freeman (Saurik), an app that
allows all users to easily install Jailbreak tweaks.
